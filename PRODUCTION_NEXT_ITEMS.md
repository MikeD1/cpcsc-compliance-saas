# Production Next Items

ComplianceOne should not compete with Excel as “13 controls in a table.” The production focus is to make it a CPCSC Level 1 readiness coach and buyer-proof generator.

## Core value to build toward

> Help Canadian defence suppliers know where they stand on CPCSC Level 1, what to fix next, and produce a credible buyer-ready readiness packet quickly.

The value is not storage. The value is confidence, judgment, prioritization, and a usable proof artifact.

## Production priorities

### 1. Add a “what good looks like” layer

For each CPCSC Level 1 control, provide practical guidance that a spreadsheet does not provide.

Build:

- Plain-English control goal.
- Examples of acceptable evidence.
- Weak vs strong implementation-note examples.
- Common mistakes.
- “A buyer/security reviewer may ask…” prompts.
- Suggested next action when the control is incomplete.

Success criteria:

- A user can open a control and understand what to do without needing a consultant for basic orientation.
- Guidance remains careful: no certification, audit, government approval, or assessor-equivalence claims.

### 2. Upgrade the dashboard into a readiness diagnosis

The dashboard should explain why the organization is or is not buyer-ready, not only show counts.

Build:

- Readiness narrative: “You are/are not buyer-ready because…”
- Strongest areas.
- Riskiest gaps.
- Top 3 recommended next moves.
- Evidence-quality warnings, not only evidence presence.
- Clear path from diagnosis to the exact control/evidence action.

Success criteria:

- The dashboard answers: “What should I do next, and why?”
- The user can move from uncertainty to action in under 10 minutes.

### 3. Make the report consultant-grade while staying honest

The PDF/report is the paid artifact. It should feel like a credible readiness assessment snapshot, not a data export.

Build:

- Executive readiness narrative.
- Readiness confidence level with explanation.
- Priority gaps and business impact.
- Suggested action plan.
- Evidence quality notes.
- Control-by-control appendix.
- Strong disclaimers: internal readiness snapshot only; not certification, audit, government acceptance, procurement eligibility, or assessor approval.

Success criteria:

- A user would feel comfortable using the report in an internal leadership, buyer, partner, or procurement-readiness conversation.
- The report is valuable even before every control is complete because it clearly shows progress and gaps.

### 4. Create a sample completed workspace and sample report

Sales needs a concrete artifact that makes the value obvious before signup.

Build:

- Sample organization/workspace data.
- Completed and partially completed control examples.
- Sample evidence-register entries.
- Sample buyer-ready readiness report/PDF.
- Public marketing preview that explains “before ComplianceOne” vs “after ComplianceOne.”

Success criteria:

- A prospect can see the sample and immediately understand why ComplianceOne is more useful than a spreadsheet.
- The sample demonstrates confidence and proof, not just tracking.

### 5. Validate with real prospects before broadening scope

Do not add broad GRC, file vault, multi-framework support, or heavy onboarding until the narrow value is validated.

Run:

- 10–15 discovery calls with Canadian defence suppliers, consultants, or procurement-adjacent contacts.
- 3–5 design partner/private beta conversations.
- Pricing validation for roughly $149–$349 CAD/month.
- Objection log focused on: Excel replacement, buyer conversation value, trust, evidence expectations, and willingness to pay.

Ask:

1. Would this replace your spreadsheet or readiness doc?
2. Would this help before a buyer/security/procurement conversation?
3. Is the report useful enough to pay for?
4. What would make you trust it?
5. Would you pay $149–$349 CAD/month for this? If not, why?

Success criteria:

- At least 3 real prospects say the report/workflow would help them prepare for a buyer or procurement conversation.
- At least 1–2 prospects are willing to become design partners or pay for early access.

## Do not prioritize yet

Avoid these until the core wedge is proven:

- Broad GRC platform features.
- CPCSC Level 2/3 expansion.
- Multi-framework compliance mapping.
- Full file-vault/storage system.
- Heavy onboarding wizard.
- Complex admin console.
- Custom enterprise workflows.

## Positioning reminder

Sell:

> CPCSC Level 1 readiness confidence and buyer-ready proof.

Do not sell:

> A checklist database.

Excel can store rows. ComplianceOne must package judgment, direction, and a credible artifact.
