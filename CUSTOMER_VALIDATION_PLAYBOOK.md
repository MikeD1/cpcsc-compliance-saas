# Customer Validation Playbook

ComplianceOne’s next risk is not whether the 13 controls can be tracked. Excel can do that. The validation question is whether prospects value the judgment layer: readiness diagnosis, buyer-conversation confidence, evidence guidance, and the report artifact.

## Target prospects

Prioritize:

- Canadian defence suppliers preparing for buyer/procurement/security conversations.
- Small and mid-sized suppliers without a mature compliance team.
- Consultants or advisors who help suppliers prepare for security/compliance questions.
- Procurement-adjacent contacts who understand what buyers ask for.

## Validation goal

Book 10–15 conversations and identify 3–5 potential design partners.

A strong signal is not “this looks nice.” A strong signal is:

- They currently use a spreadsheet/doc/shared drive for this work.
- They are unsure what “good enough” looks like.
- They need a credible artifact for a buyer, partner, leadership, or procurement conversation.
- They would pay, pilot, or introduce another qualified prospect.

## Outreach message

Subject: Quick question on CPCSC Level 1 readiness

Hi {{name}},

I’m building ComplianceOne, a lightweight CPCSC Level 1 readiness workspace for Canadian defence suppliers.

The goal is not another checklist. It helps a team understand where they stand, what evidence is missing, what to fix next, and produce a buyer-ready readiness snapshot before customer/procurement/security conversations.

Could I ask you for 20 minutes of feedback on the sample report and workflow?

I’m especially trying to learn whether this would replace the spreadsheet/doc process teams use today, and whether the report is useful enough to pay for.

Thanks,
Mike

## Discovery call script

### 1. Context

- What kind of defence/procurement/security conversations are you preparing for?
- Are CPCSC Level 1 requirements currently on your radar?
- Who owns this work internally today?

### 2. Current workflow

- How are you tracking readiness now?
- Is it a spreadsheet, Word doc, shared drive, consultant deliverable, email thread, or something else?
- What breaks or gets messy in that workflow?
- What takes the most time?

### 3. Confidence gap

- Do you know what “good enough” evidence looks like for each control?
- What would make you feel confident before a buyer/security/procurement conversation?
- What would make you nervous sending a readiness summary to someone else?

### 4. Show sample report/workflow

Use:

- `/sample-report`
- `/buyer-packet`
- dashboard readiness diagnosis
- control “what good looks like” guidance

Ask:

- What is immediately clear?
- What is confusing?
- What is missing before you would trust this?
- Would this help you explain readiness to leadership or a buyer?

### 5. Pricing and willingness to pay

- Would this replace your current spreadsheet/doc?
- Would you pay $149 CAD/month for this?
- Would you pay $349 CAD/month if multiple people needed to collaborate and report regularly?
- If not, what would need to be true?
- Would a one-time setup/review package be more attractive?

### 6. Close

- Would you be willing to pilot this with a real workspace?
- Who else should I talk to?
- Can I follow up after I update the sample report or workflow?

## Signals to watch

### Strong buy signals

- “We have this in a spreadsheet and it is messy.”
- “I need something I can show leadership/customer/procurement.”
- “I don’t know what evidence is enough.”
- “Can you help us set this up?”
- “Can I try this with our real data?”

### Weak signals

- “Looks interesting.”
- “Maybe useful someday.”
- “Send me a link” with no specific use case.
- They only care about file storage or broad GRC features.

### Product risks to validate

- Is CPCSC Level 1 urgent enough right now?
- Is the report artifact valuable enough to pay for?
- Is the guidance trusted without a human consultant attached?
- Does the buyer/security language feel credible?
- Is register-only evidence acceptable before file uploads exist?

## Decision rule

After 10–15 conversations:

Proceed if:

- At least 3 prospects say the workflow/report would help with a real buyer/procurement/security conversation.
- At least 1–2 prospects agree to pilot or pay for early access.
- The top objections are fixable within the narrow CPCSC Level 1 wedge.

Pause or reposition if:

- Most prospects say Excel is enough.
- The problem is not urgent.
- The report is not useful without consultant involvement.
- Buyers require proof or integrations the product cannot provide soon.
